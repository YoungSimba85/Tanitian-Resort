Visitor Information

Everything you need to know for a safe and enjoyable stay on Taniti.

Safety & Crime Rates

Taniti is considered a safe destination for travelers. Crime rates are low and violent crime is rare. Visitors are encouraged to take normal precautions, such as safeguarding valuables and staying aware of surroundings at night.

  • Emergency Number: 911
  • Police Headquarters: 123 Island Road
  • Resort Security available 24/7

Health & Medical Services

The island offers modern, reliable medical services. The main hospital is equipped for emergencies, while several clinics handle non-urgent care.

  • Taniti General Hospital – 2 miles from downtown
  • Island Urgent Care Clinic – Open 8am–10pm
  • Pharmacies located throughout the central district

Travel & Transportation

Getting around Taniti is simple and traveler-friendly. The island offers multiple transportation options that are safe and easy to use.

  • Airport Shuttle to major resorts
  • Taxi and rideshare services available island-wide
  • Public bus routes operate daily from 6am–11pm
  • Car, scooter, and bicycle rentals available

Local Essentials

Whether you need groceries, banking, or everyday necessities, Taniti provides easy access to essentials.

  • Grocery Stores: Island Market, Ocean Fresh Foods
  • Convenience Stores: Located near major hotels and attractions
  • Hospitals & Clinics: See Health section
  • Banks & ATMs: Available throughout downtown
  • Gas Stations: Located along the coastal highway

Weather & Climate

Taniti enjoys warm tropical weather year-round. Expect temperatures between 75–88°F (24–31°C). Rainfall is more common from May to September, but storms are brief and the island remains sunny most days.

Local Customs & Etiquette

Taniti locals are friendly and welcoming. A few simple customs will help you enjoy your visit:

  • Greetings are usually a smile or light handshake.
  • Tipping is appreciated but not required; 10% is typical.
  • Beachwear is for beaches—casual clothing is expected in town.

Resort Policies

  • Check-in: 3:00 PM
  • Check-out: 11:00 AM
  • No smoking inside buildings
  • Quiet hours: 10 PM – 7 AM
  • Pets allowed in designated areas only

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of power outlets does Taniti use?

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same as in the United States.

What are the alcohol laws in Taniti?

Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m.

The drinking age is 18, and enforcement is not very strict.

Do people in Taniti speak English?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially among older residents.

How is medical care on the island?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

Is Taniti safe for visitors?

Violent crime is very rare. However, as tourism grows, there are more reports of pickpocketing and petty theft.

Are attractions open year-round?

Taniti has many national holidays. On these holidays, tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed, so plan accordingly.

What currency does Taniti use?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, but many businesses also accept euros and yen.

Banks offer currency exchange services, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
